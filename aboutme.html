Unreal, a paradigm shift

It was indeed, back when I was introduced to it in March, 2014.

Although I was a gamer, it had never occurred to me about knowing the world of game development. As a child I always used to think that methods of game development are company's top secret which they do not reveal to the general masses, and the secret of making a game will not be known to an enthusiastic person unless he/she joins the gaming industry.

My misconception was partly cleared when I was showing interest and getting my hands dirty with several 3D Modelling and Animation Tools, and back in 2012-2013, when I came across with Cryengine and UDK, which was completely free for everyone, imagine the excitement I started to have. I was blown away by the beautiful graphics of Cryenigne ,whether it is the volumetric Clouds, or the amazing water shader, or the foliage and the world building tools that was letting me create level comparable to the graphics of games like Farcry, within minutes. Although Cryengine was amazing in getting started with outdoor environment building, I found UDK to be much easier and simpler, but not as good in terms of graphics. The comicky outlook irrespective the type of environment I wanted to create was always bothering me. These all were before Unreal Engine 4 was launched, in March, 2014. Ever since then, there was no reason to look back.
